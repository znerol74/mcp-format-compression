# How to Write a Paper

## Introduction Structure

1. **Motivation** — Why does this problem matter? Set the scene.
2. **Research Gap** — What is missing in current work?
3. **Objective** — What does this paper aim to do?
4. **Method/Approach** — How do we do it? Research questions.
5. **Findings** — What did we find? Key results.
6. **Contribution** — What do we give to the field?
7. **Impact** — What does this enable?

## Figure Captions

1. **Title** — Summarizes what the figure shows.
2. **How to read** — Describes what we see and how to interpret it.
3. **Observation** — What we observe in the data.
4. **Meaning** — What this means.

Keep it short overall.

## Writing Style

- No semicolons
- No em dashes
- Frame unverified claims as hypotheses, not assertions
- Keep explanations concise, avoid repetitive summaries
- Use flowing sentences with embedded clauses, not choppy bullet-point prose
